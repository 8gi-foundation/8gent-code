# How The Circle Works - Voiceover Script
**Presenter:** Daniel, 8EO (8gent Executive Officer)
**Voice:** `say -v Daniel`
**Slides:** 6

---

## Slide 1 - Cover
How the circle works. I'm Daniel, the 8gent Executive Officer, and I'm going to walk you through the governance charter that keeps this collective organised, fair, and scalable.

## Slide 2 - The Four Tiers
Four tiers of membership. The Founder. That's James. One permanent seat. Veto power on constitutional matters, standard vote on operational ones. Core members. Three to twelve people with full voting rights. They review PRs, maintain packages, mentor contributors, attend quarterly sync. Contributors. Unlimited, quality-gated. They submit PRs, follow the two hundred line discipline, and have advisory votes. Observers. Also unlimited. They watch, learn, and decide whether to commit. No vote. No obligation.

## Slide 3 - The Vouch System
Nobody walks in off the street. Observers can be invited by any member. Contributors must be nominated by a Core member. There's a forty-eight hour objection window. Then a thirty day probation period where only the Founder reviews their PRs. Core members need six months as a Contributor, ten merged PRs minimum, and Founder approval. We vet on four criteria. Technical competence. Constitutional alignment. Collaborative track record. And motivation. Are you here to build, or to extract?

## Slide 4 - Voting Mechanics
Four categories of decisions. Constitutional matters need the Founder's veto plus seventy-five percent Core supermajority. Strategic decisions need a simple majority with the Founder breaking ties. Operational decisions are Core simple majority. Community matters are advisory only. All votes happen asynchronously on Telegram. Seventy-two hours for operational, seven days for strategic and constitutional.

## Slide 5 - PR Review Scaling
This scales with trust. Phase one, one to five members, only the Founder merges. Phase two, five to fifteen, the Founder plus Core members merge. Phase three, fifteen to fifty, Core members merge and the Founder reviews architecture-level PRs only. Phase four, fifty plus, domain ownership with designated maintainers for each package. Every review checks constitution compliance, two hundred line discipline, security, tests, and documentation.

## Slide 6 - Scaling Phases
Five phases of growth. Seed, one to five members, the Founder decides everything. Growth, five to fifteen, the Core circle is active, review is distributed. Scale, fifteen to fifty, domain sub-teams form with elected leads. Network, fifty to two hundred, federated governance with regional circles. Movement, two hundred to five hundred plus, constitutional democracy with elected representatives. The key principle: trust scales slower than code. We never add members faster than the circle can vet and mentor them.
